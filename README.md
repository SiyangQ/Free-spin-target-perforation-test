# Free-spin-target-perforation-test

This repository hosts the high-speed experiment videos corresponding to the study *“Mechanical-impulse detumble dynamics and perforation energy model for the detumble and capture of tumbling space debris”*.
The article formulates (1) dynamic equilibrium models for projectile–target impact, (2) quasi-static perforation-energy models for petaling and plugging failures in aluminum honeycomb sandwich panels, and (3) a novel free-spin-target impact test methodology.

These videos provide direct experimental validation of the models and illustrate the perforation process and angular momentum transfer during projectile impacts against spinning targets.

## Test rig

The **free-spin-target perforation test rig** was designed to emulate in-orbit projectile strikes against tumbling debris in a controlled ground environment.

* **Target system**: Aluminum honeycomb sandwich panels mounted on a rotating frame with one DoF about the vertical axis. A drive motor accelerates the target before disengagement, allowing free-spin motion at prescribed angular velocities.
* **Projectile launcher**: Air cannon capable of firing six different projectile head geometries (flat, v-groove, conical 20°, conical 45°, ogive, spherical) at 20–40 m/s.
* **Recording**: A high-speed camera positioned above the target captures impact and perforation dynamics.

*Please visit the article for detailed description of the system configuration, target materials, and projectiles.*

## Experiment procedure

The experiments followed a structured series of stationary and rotating target tests to calibrate and validate the analytical models:

1. **Stationary orthogonal impact tests** – Determine perforation energy modification factors.
2. **Stationary off-center impacts** – Transfer angular momentum with varying lever arms.
3. **Rotating target impacts** – Verify detumble predictions across different initial angular velocities.
4. **Dual impacts** – Explore interaction effects of adjacent impacts (petaling vs. plugging).

*A total of 44 tests were conducted, and their test videos are presented in this repository.*

## Video usage

* Videos recorded at **1000 frames per second**, resolution **640 × 480**.
* Experiments conducted over **four days**. Each day included a calibration video with a centimeter ruler at launcher-center height.
* **Total tests**: 44.
* **Projectiles**: 6 types, equal diameter/mass, varying nose geometries.
* **Target conditions**: Stationary and multiple initial angular velocities.
